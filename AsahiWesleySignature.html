Japanese Food Culture

Japanese food is very different compared to American dishes in terms of authenticity. In Japan, most food contains soybeans
or broth, which makes the dish so flavorful and authentic.

One famous Japanese dish is fermented soybeans which we eat with rice and other Japanese dishes. We have different soybeans and broth dishes,
such as Miso soup or Tofu.

Japan doesn't have a combination of different countries' cuisines compared to the US since the culture itself is different from the US.

American Food Culture

The culture of the food in the U.S. can best be described as undefined. With influences from the melting pot of nationalities
and cultures that make up the population, there are hundreds of various cuisines, each with their own unique flavors.

With that said, there are also foods that many would identify as "American," such as the hamburger and hotdog. Although how well
these foods are accepted in the international community compared to how well foreign foods are accepted in the United States
is another matter.

However, let's take a look at how two seemingly different cuisines—Japanese and American—and some recipes to go along with them,
delving into the commonalities between the two! Enjoy this culinary journey!
